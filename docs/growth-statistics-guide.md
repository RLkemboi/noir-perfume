# NOIR Growth Statistics Guide

This guide explains how to read the admin financial dashboard and use it to grow the business instead of just observing numbers.

## 1. The Order of Importance

Look at the numbers in this order:

1. `Realized Revenue`
This is actual money secured.
If this is weak while booked revenue is high, collection is failing.

2. `Outstanding COD`
This is revenue at risk.
If it rises too fast, tighten COD follow-up and review customer payment behaviour.

3. `Cancelled Revenue`
This is direct leakage.
If it spikes, inspect delivery delays, poor stock flow, or weak customer intent.

4. `Estimated Gross Profit`
This tells you whether growth is productive.
Revenue growth without profit growth is not healthy scaling.

5. `Weekly Profit Movement`
This tells you whether the business improved or weakened versus last week.
Green weeks are stronger operating weeks. Red weeks mean the business consumed more value than it captured that week.

6. `Average Review Rating`
A review drop usually shows future refund risk, lower repeat buying, and weaker referrals.

## 2. What Each Core Metric Means

### Booked Revenue
Value of active orders placed.
Use it to measure demand.
Do not confuse it with cash collected.

### Realized Revenue
Actual payments recorded.
Use it for cash health, spending decisions, and weekly confidence.

### Pipeline Value
Orders still moving through the system.
Use it to forecast upcoming fulfillment pressure.

### COD Exposure
Unpaid balance on pay-on-delivery orders.
Use it to judge collection risk.
If COD exposure rises faster than realized revenue, tighten COD limits or push payment prompts earlier.

### Cancelled Revenue
Lost value from cancelled orders.
Use it to find process leakage.
Investigate whether cancellations come from late delivery, low trust, or poor order quality.

### Estimated Gross Profit
Booked revenue minus estimated shipped-cost burden.
Use it to judge whether order growth is worth the operational cost.

### Net Worth Proxy
An operating proxy based on realized cash, shipped expense recognition, and cancellation drag.
Use it as a directional business health signal, not audited accounting.

### Units Sold
Total item units moved.
Use it to spot whether revenue growth came from higher volume or only higher ticket size.

### Average Order Value
Average revenue per active order.
Use it to evaluate bundling, upsells, and premium positioning.

### Delivery Completion Rate
Percent of active orders that reached delivery.
Use it to watch operational execution quality.

### Average Review Rating
Post-delivery sentiment quality.
Use it as an early warning for brand damage.

## 3. How to Use the Weekly Graph

The graph shows up to 8 weeks of movement.

- `Green week`
Weekly profit improved versus the previous week.

- `Red week`
Weekly profit fell versus the previous week.

- `Yellow / flat`
Little or no change.

Read the graph together with these questions:

1. Did realized revenue increase?
2. Did shipped expense grow faster than revenue?
3. Did cancellations rise?
4. Did COD balances stay unpaid longer?

If profit falls while booked revenue rises, the business is likely growing inefficiently.

## 4. What To Check Daily

Every day:

1. Look at `Outstanding COD`.
2. Review `Partial` and `Unpaid` COD counts.
3. Review recent customer comments and low-star reviews.
4. Watch orders stuck before delivery confirmation.

Daily focus is cash collection and issue resolution.

## 5. What To Check Weekly

Every week:

1. Compare weekly profit trend to the previous week.
2. Compare booked revenue to realized revenue.
3. Compare cancellations to total booked revenue.
4. Compare units sold to average order value.
5. Review customer rating trend.

Weekly focus is growth quality, not just sales volume.

## 6. What Actions Good Operators Take

If `Outstanding COD` is high:
- Push payment prompts sooner.
- Reduce COD usage for weak tiers or risky customer behaviour.
- Watch delivery-agent follow-through.

If `Cancelled Revenue` is high:
- Audit delivery times.
- Check address quality.
- Inspect whether customers are abandoning COD obligations.

If `Average Order Value` is low:
- Improve bundles.
- Raise premium product visibility.
- Offer curated pairings or discovery add-ons.

If `Units Sold` are high but profit is weak:
- The team may be discounting or over-serving low-margin orders.
- Push premium mixes, not just volume.

If `Average Review Rating` falls:
- Read comments immediately.
- Separate product dissatisfaction from delivery dissatisfaction.
- Fix the failure with the strongest frequency first.

## 7. Who Should Manage What

### Admin
Owns all financial oversight.
Reviews revenue, COD exposure, cancellations, weekly profit trend, and final delivery confirmation.

### Operations
Owns order flow speed and successful movement from processing to shipped to delivered.
Should not own final financial reconciliation.

### Delivery Team
Owns handoff completion and customer communication.
Should help reduce failed COD collections by confirming delivery quality and payment readiness.

### Marketing
Uses order, review, and revenue trends for growth campaigns.
Should react to average order value, repeat-quality signals, and low-rating patterns.

## 8. Best Growth Mindset

Do not celebrate booked revenue alone.
Strong businesses grow:

- realized cash
- gross profit
- trusted delivery
- strong reviews
- repeat-worthy customer experience

That is the scoreboard that matters.
